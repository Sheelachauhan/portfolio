Mohandas Karamchandra Gandhi

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi (2 October 1869- 30 January 1948) was an Indian lawyer, anti-colonial nationalist, and political ethicist who employed nonviolent resistance to lead the successful campaign for India's independence from British rule. He inspired movements for civil rights and freedom across the world. The honorific Mahātmā (from Sanskrit, meaning great-souled, or venerable), first applied to him in South Africa in 1914, is now used throughout the world.[3]

Biographies

  • Marriage: Married Kasturba Gandhi at the age of 13
  • Education: Attended primary and high school in Rajkot, and then Samaldas College in Bhavnagar
  • Law studies: Studied law in England, and then practiced law in Bombay and Rajkot
  • South Africa: Moved to South Africa in 1893 to practice law, and organized the Natal Indian Congress
  • Return to India: Returned to India in 1901 with his family
